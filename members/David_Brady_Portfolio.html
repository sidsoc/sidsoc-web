Project Overview

Secure Network Solutions is the name of the business which my fellow teammates and I created as part of our Business Entrepreneurship module in the second year of my Computing degree. As part of this module, we were tasked with creating an innovative business product and constructing a business plan which could be used in order to achieve financial backing for the project. The business plan earned one of the highest grades out of all participating team projects across both the School of Business and the School of Computing and which resulted in our team being shortlisted for the prestigious NCI Dean's Award finals. The Secure Network Solutions team went on to successfully win the NCI Dean's Award in May 2014.

My Role

My role on this project was to devise the marketing section of the plan. This consisted of: market overview and trend analysis, competitor analysis, customer segmentation, SWOT analysis and marketing strategy.

The Concept (The SNS-1)

The concept behind Secure Network Solutions was to provide a hardware device that would house anti-virus software and would reside between the user's ISP source and their network router. The idea is that this would allow for data to be filtered at source so that any data propagated from the router would be secure. This would then eliminate the need for individual anti-virus licences on individual devices connected to the network.
