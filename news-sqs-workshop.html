SQS - Software Testing Workshop Published on: 25/01/15

On 19th November 2014, Steve Hall, Software Test Lead at SQS, hosted an engaging and interactive workshop on software testing. This workshop was part of the “Skills Gap” series and was well attended with over 40 NCI students present from both full-time and part-time courses. Steve strongly encouraged students to consider this area as a career choice and highlighted the benefits of software testing ahead of a product launch.

Interactive Workshop

The three-hour workshop started off as a career talk and overview of the role of a software tester but quickly became an engaging, hands-on interactive session. Students learned about unit and functional testing throughout the development lifecyle and had the opportunity to write their own test automation scripts at the end.
Many students told us that before attending the workshop they had never considered a career in software testing but will be looking into it further as a result of attending the workshop.

Skills Gap Series

This workshop was delivered as part of SID Soc's "Skills Gap" series. The purpose of this series is to identify, with the help of the NCI Careers Department, skills that employers are looking for in graduate applicants but that might not be addressed in the current college modules.
